# IBM SkillsBuild – TORCS Autonomous Driving Learning Lab

## Overview

Welcome to the **IBM SkillsBuild TORCS Autonomous Driving Learning Lab**.

This repository hosts a hands‑on learning lab designed to help students and aspiring developers explore **autonomous driving concepts using simulation**. You will work with **TORCS (The Open Racing Car Simulator)** and a Python‑based AI driver to understand how autonomous systems sense their environment, make decisions, and act in real time.

This is a **learning lab**, not a competition.  
There are no leaderboards, prizes, or “winning” solutions. The goal is to **learn by building, observing, experimenting, and reflecting**.

***

## Who this repository is for

This repository is intended for:

*   Students exploring AI, software development, or simulation
*   Aspiring developers looking for hands‑on, portfolio‑ready projects
*   University clubs and self‑learners
*   Educators seeking practical AI teaching material

You do not need prior experience with:

*   Racing simulators
*   Robotics
*   Autonomous vehicles

Basic familiarity with Python is helpful but not required.

***

## What you will learn

By completing the lab in this repository, you will:

*   Run an autonomous AI driver in a simulation environment
*   Understand how sensor data is used to control steering, throttle, and braking
*   Modify Python code that controls a simulated vehicle
*   Experiment with control parameters and observe real‑time effects
*   Learn how simulation is used in real‑world AI development
*   Document and share your learning through a GitHub repository

***

***

## How to use this repository

1.  **Fork this repository** to your own GitHub account
2.  Navigate to `lab/01_torcs_lab/README_LAB.md`
3.  Begin with `00_intro.md`, then follow `01_torcs_lab.md` step by step
4.  Run the simulator and modify the AI driver
5.  Record your observations in `RESULTS.md`
6.  Commit and publish your work in your fork

Your fork becomes a **public record of your hands‑on learning**.

***

## About TORCS in this lab

TORCS is used here strictly as a **learning simulator**, not as a game or competition platform.

*   You do not need to modify the TORCS engine
*   You will focus on Python‑based AI behavior
*   Setup instructions are provided as guides, separate from the lab itself
*   Simulation lets you experiment safely and iteratively

If something does not work during setup, refer to the setup guides rather than skipping ahead.

## Learning philosophy

This lab is designed around a few core principles:

*   **Learning over performance**
*   **Experimentation over correctness**
*   **Understanding over optimization**
*   **Reflection over results**

If your car spins out, goes off track, or behaves unexpectedly — that’s part of the learning.
